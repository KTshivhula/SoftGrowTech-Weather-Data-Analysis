# 🌦️ Weather Intelligence for Agricultural Logistics  
### *Turning 150,000+ weather records into actionable supply chain decisions*

---

## 🎯 What This Project Solves

An agricultural logistics company struggles with **crop spoilage, delivery delays, and driver safety** due to unpredictable weather. They need to understand *historical weather patterns* to make smarter, long‑term decisions—not just react to daily forecasts.

**I delivered a complete, end‑to‑end data analysis that answers 5 critical business questions with clear visual evidence and actionable recommendations.**

---

## 🔍 Key Business Questions Answered

| # | Question | Insight |
| :---: | :--- | :--- |
| 1️⃣ | Which regions have the most extreme daily temperature swings? | **Bolivia (10.6°C)** and **USA (10.0°C)** – target for insulated packaging |
| 2️⃣ | Does humidity predict rainfall? | **Weak correlation (r = 0.15)** – use direct forecasts, not humidity proxies |
| 3️⃣ | When are the hottest and coldest months globally? | **July (26°C)** and **January (16°C)** – align maintenance and harvest schedules |
| 4️⃣ | How does wind vary by climate zone? | **Polar zones** have highest median winds (18.7 kph) – need stricter safety rules |
| 5️⃣ | What seasonal patterns exist? | **Strong temperature signal**; precipitation, humidity and wind are stable year‑round |
